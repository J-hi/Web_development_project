ORGAN AND TISSUE DONATION

WHAT IS ORGAN AND TISSUE DONATION?

Organ donation is the gift of an organ to a person with end-stage organ disease and who needs a transplant. The donated organ may be from a deceased donor or a living donor.

WHAT IS TRANSPLANTATION?

Surgical operation in which a failing or damaged organ in the human body is removed and replaced with a functioning one.

WHO CAN PLEDGE TO DONATE ORGANS?

Any person not less than 18 years of age, who voluntarily authorizes the removal of any of their organs or tissues after death.

About Organ Donation

Organ donation is a selfless act that can save lives. Here’s what you need to know about the process and how you can get involved.

Why Organ Donation Matters

Organ donation matters because it saves lives. Every year, thousands of people need organ transplants, and each donor can save multiple lives.

How Does It Work?

The process involves registering as a donor, and in the event of brain death, doctors will evaluate the viability of the organs for transplant.

Common Myths & Facts

There are many myths about organ donation, but the truth is that almost anyone, regardless of age or medical history, can become a donor.
